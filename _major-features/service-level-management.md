# 8. Service Level Management (SLM)

**Defining and Meeting Service Expectations**

## Major Features

| Feature | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Service Level Agreement (SLA) Definition** | Tools to define service targets, metrics, and reporting periods. |
| **SLA Monitoring & Reporting** | Tracking performance against defined SLAs and generating reports for stakeholders. |
| **Service Level Objectives (SLO) & Operational Level Agreements (OLA)** | Supporting the definition and management of internal agreements that contribute to overall SLAs. |
| **Dashboards and reports on service performance against targets** | |

## ITIL 4 Process Alignment

| Practice | Alignment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Service Level Management** | Directly supports this process by providing the framework and tools to negotiate, agree, monitor, and report on service levels. |
| **Service Design** | Informing the creation and definition of services and their associated levels. |
| **Continual Improvement** | SLA performance data drives improvement initiatives. |
| **Service Level Management Practice** | The software automates the tracking and reporting that this practice requires. |
| **Service Value Chain - Plan & Improve** | Provides the critical feedback loop on whether services are meeting business needs. |
| **Enabling Features that Support the ITIL 4 SVS** | These are cross-cutting features that empower the entire ITSM system and align with ITIL 4's guiding principles. |
